# Announcing Our Community Investment Round on WeFunder

We're EV drivers. We know what's broken.

We've spent years pulling into charging stations and finding the same thing: parking lots, broken equipment, no restrooms, and nowhere to wait except our cars. We've sat through summer heat and winter rain, hoping the charger actually works, counting down the minutes until we can leave.

We knew there had to be something better. So we're building it.

## Why We're Raising From the Community

Rangeway is building America's first hospitality-driven EV charging network. Every location includes a climate-controlled Driver's Lounge with premium restrooms, comfortable seating, and high-speed Wi-Fi. Not as an upgrade. As the standard.

We think like hotel operators, not utility companies.

When it came time to raise capital for our first location, we had a choice. We could go the traditional route: pitch venture capitalists, take their money, answer to their timeline. Or we could do something different.

We chose different.

The EV community has been asking for better charging infrastructure for years. We think that same community should have the opportunity to own a piece of what we're building. Built by EV drivers, for EV drivers, owned by them too.

## The Opportunity

In Q1 2026, we're opening a community investment round on WeFunder. We're raising between $1M and $5M through a debt note offering 9% annual return, secured by project assets and state rebates.

This funding will advance our first Waystation location in the Western United States to construction, with doors opening in 2027.

Here's what your investment supports:

**Site Development:** Site control, permitting, engineering, and environmental reviews.

**Infrastructure Planning:** Utility coordination, equipment specifications, solar canopy and battery storage systems.

**Team Building:** Key leadership hires to execute our vision.

**Go-to-Market:** Brand launch, Pathfinder Rewards platform, and strategic partnerships.

## How It Works

Your investment is secured by four revenue streams: charging fees, California LCFS credits, automated retail, and state grants. When state rebates come through, they're applied as principal prepayment, returning capital to you faster.

The term is 84 months (7 years), with annual payments beginning after construction is complete.

This is a chance to earn a solid return while helping build infrastructure that actually respects your time.

## What Comes Next

With this funding, our first Waystation opens in 2027. From there, we expand across the West with Basecamps and Waystations, bringing hospitality-driven charging to the routes where drivers actually need it.

We're building the charging network we always wanted. We'd love for you to own a piece of it.
