When we set out to build Rangeway, we started with a simple belief: EV charging should be part of the journey, not an interruption to it. That philosophy shapes everything we do, from the climate-controlled Driver's Lounges at every location to the quality coffee waiting inside.

It should also shape how we look.

Today, we're introducing a refreshed Rangeway brand identity. But before I show you where we landed, I want to share how we got here.

## Where We Started

Our original logo featured dark mountain silhouettes against a warm amber-orange gradient sky, with a pale sun rising in the corner. The colors were warm, the execution was clean, and as an app icon it looked professional.

The problem was what it communicated.

When people saw that logo, they thought "outdoor brand." They thought trail apps, hiking guides, national park services. The mountain imagery said "wilderness" and "adventure" when we needed it to say "welcome" and "comfort."

We're not an outdoor brand. We're a hospitality company. We happen to serve travelers on highways that pass through beautiful places, but our promise isn't adventure. Our promise is comfort. The warm glow of a Driver's Lounge after a long stretch of desert highway. The relief of stepping into climate control on a 110-degree afternoon. A clean restroom, a quality cup of coffee, a place designed for humans rather than just vehicles.

The mountain logo didn't capture any of that.

## The Realization

The turning point came when we started describing what we do to potential partners and investors. We'd explain the indoor comfort guarantee, the hospitality-first philosophy, the way we think like hotel operators rather than utility companies. They'd get it. They'd be excited.

Then they'd look at our logo and say:

> "Oh, so you're focused on national parks? Like a trail guide for EV drivers?"

Not exactly. Yes, many of our locations will be near parks and natural destinations. But that's not what we're about. We're not selling the wilderness. We're selling the warm place you stop on the way there.

We realized our visual identity was working against us. Every conversation started with correcting a first impression. We were spending energy explaining what we weren't instead of showing what we are.

Something had to change.

## Finding Our Mark

We explored several directions. Some leaned further into the outdoor aesthetic, reasoning that maybe we just needed to execute it better. Others went minimal and abstract, the kind of geometric logomarks that tech companies favor.

None of them felt right.

The breakthrough came when we stopped asking "what do we look like?" and started asking "what do we want people to feel when they see us from the highway?"

The answer: relief. Anticipation. The sense that comfort is just ahead.

That's when the Path Mark emerged.

At first glance, it's a distinctive "R." Look closer, and you'll see a road extending forward, with a warm dot marking your destination.

That warmth isn't decorative. It represents the glow you see from the highway: the lit window of a Driver's Lounge, the signal that this stop will be different from the cold, utilitarian charging experiences you've had before.

It says "destination" rather than "wilderness." It signals premium without pretension. It's unmistakably ours.

**Typography** was the next piece. Our original wordmark used Montserrat, a solid choice that countless brands rely on. But solid wasn't what we needed. We needed refined.

Raleway Bold brought exactly the right character. It's elegant without being delicate, modern without chasing trends. The distinctive crossed W adds personality. The slight negative letter-spacing gives it a confident, grounded presence that Montserrat lacked.

The moment we set the wordmark in Raleway, the difference was obvious. This was a hospitality brand, not a tech company.

**Color** came next. Our palette was already warm, drawing from golden hour and the promise of a comfortable stop ahead. Charcoal grounds everything. Warm (our signature amber) signals destination and welcome. Cream provides breathing room. Sage adds depth for campaigns and seasonal moments.

But we added something new: **Beacon**.

This deep teal bridges our warm hospitality palette with the functional elements of our charging infrastructure. When you pull into a Rangeway location, you'll see Beacon blue on our charging dispensers, topped with that signature warm orange cap. It's the color of wayfinding, of "this way to comfort." It connects the physical charging experience to the hospitality promise waiting inside.

## What It All Means

A brand refresh might seem like a small thing when you're building physical infrastructure. Chargers, buildings, real estate, permits. Those are the hard parts.

But identity matters. First impressions matter. The feeling someone gets when they see your sign from a quarter mile away matters.

Our old logo made people think we sold hiking gear. Our new mark makes them think about a warm place to rest. That's not a subtle difference. That's the entire point.

We think like hotel operators, not utility companies. We guarantee indoor comfort at every location, an industry first. We believe a 20-minute charging stop can be a moment worth savoring rather than time to endure.

Now our visual identity tells that story too.

## The Road Ahead

This refresh coincides with an exciting chapter for Rangeway. As we prepare to open our first locations and welcome our first Pathfinder Rewards members, we wanted our visual identity to match the experience we're building.

When you see the Path Mark on a highway sign, you'll know exactly what's waiting: a Driver's Lounge with premium restrooms, comfortable seating, quality refreshments, and reliable charging. The warm dot is a promise.

We're grateful to everyone who has supported us on this journey so far. Whether you're a future guest, a partner, an investor, or simply someone who believes EV travel should be better, this new brand is for you.

The road ahead looks bright. We hope you'll join us.
